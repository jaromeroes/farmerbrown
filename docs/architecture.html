03 / coi_flow_detail
step.01 · policyholder
Confirm caller identity using the phone number on file. If different, ask for business name and the phone on file.
step.02 · additional insured
Collect name, street, city, state, ZIP of the party to be listed on the certificate. Slow readback to confirm before continuing.
step.03 · endorsements
Walk through three common options — waiver of subrogation, primary & non-contributory, products & completed operations — one by one.
step.04 · contact
Capture a phone or email for the additional insured when available, so the certificate can be sent directly.
step.05 · turn-around
Standard 24h. Expedited 1h offered as a quid pro quo: a review from the caller in return. If accepted: SMS review link + urgent internal alert.
step.06 · cross-sell
Ask about Home & Auto. If yes, promise an SMS application link — does not hand off to Rachel, closes the call in place.

07 / design_principles
· single entry, many paths
Every caller on every site sees the same first question — "new quote or existing?" — before the flow branches by product. Predictable triage, zero dead ends.
· specialists, not generalists
Each product has its own specialist with its own script, tone, and data requirements. A Builder's Risk caller never feels like they're filling out a Home & Auto form.
· audible handoffs
Receptionists and specialists use distinct voices, so the caller audibly recognizes when they've been connected to the right expert — not just a different prompt.
· graceful escalation
Any caller who is confused, frustrated, or asking for a person is routed to a licensed agent within seconds. The system never insists on automation when a human is the right answer.
